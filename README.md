# AeroFlow

Airline Irregular Operations (IROPs) Recovery Platform

---

## Overview

AeroFlow is a concept platform designed to help airlines manage and recover from operational disruptions.

Irregular Operations (IROPs) occur when unexpected events disrupt airline schedules, such as weather delays, crew legality issues, aircraft maintenance problems, or airport congestion. These disruptions often cascade across the airline network, affecting multiple flights, crews, and passengers.

AeroFlow explores how predictive analytics and operational decision-support tools could help airline operations teams respond to disruptions faster and more effectively.

---

## Problem

Airline operations are highly interconnected systems. When one disruption occurs, it can quickly cascade across the entire network.

Common challenges during irregular operations include:

- Aircraft out-of-position
- Crew legality violations
- Flight delay cascades
- Gate conflicts
- Passenger misconnections
- Operational decision pressure on dispatchers and operations control centers

Many recovery decisions must currently be made quickly using fragmented information across multiple systems.

---

## Concept

AeroFlow aims to provide a **decision-support platform for airline operations control centers (OCC)**.

The system would analyze operational disruptions and help identify potential recovery strategies for flights, aircraft routing, and crew scheduling.

Instead of relying solely on manual coordination, AeroFlow would provide **data-driven disruption recovery insights**.
